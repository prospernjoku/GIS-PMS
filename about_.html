About Us

The Federal Ministry of Housing & Urban Development is a key government body in Nigeria, dedicated to overseeing and enhancing the nation's housing and urban development sectors. Our mission is to ensure access to affordable and quality housing for all citizens, while promoting sustainable urban growth and development across the country. We strive to create thriving communities that are well-planned, environmentally conscious, and economically vibrant.

Our History

Established in 1999, the Ministry has played a pivotal role in shaping Nigeria's housing and urban landscape. Over the years, we have implemented numerous initiatives aimed at addressing housing deficits, improving urban infrastructure, and fostering inclusive communities. Our work is guided by a commitment to transparency, efficiency, and collaboration with stakeholders across the public and private sectors.

Our Objectives

  • Affordable Housing: Facilitating access to affordable housing options for all income groups.
  • Urban Development: Promoting sustainable and well-planned urban growth.
  • Infrastructure Improvement: Enhancing urban infrastructure and services.
  • Policy Implementation: Developing and implementing effective housing and urban development policies.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Collaborating with stakeholders to achieve our goals.

Key Responsibilities

  • Developing and implementing national housing policies and programs.
  • Managing government-owned properties and assets.
  • Regulating the housing and real estate sectors.
  • Promoting public-private partnerships in housing development.
  • Ensuring compliance with building codes and standards.
  • Monitoring and evaluating the impact of housing and urban development initiatives.

Leadership

The Ministry is led by a dedicated team of professionals committed to driving positive change in the housing and urban development sectors. Our leadership brings a wealth of experience and expertise to guide our strategic direction and ensure effective implementation of our mandate.
